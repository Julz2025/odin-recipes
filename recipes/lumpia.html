Lumpia

Deep fried meat and vegetable spring rolls

I first learned how to make lumpia for a cultural event at university showcasing Filipino cuisine. This is the version I like to make for myself and loved ones. I have to say this is a hit at parties!

Ingredients:

  • 40x spring roll wrappers
  • Meat and veg filling (see below)
  • vegetable oil for frying
  • sweet chilli sauce or any other dipping sauce of your choice

    • Meat and veg mixture:

  • 500g beef and pork mince
  • 1x large brown onion finely diced
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 3x medium sized carrots finely grated
  • 4x spring onion finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1/t tsp five spice powder
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1x large egg

Steps:

  1. Mix mince meat, diced onion, grated carrots, chopped spring onion in a large bowl.
  2. Crack one egg into the bowl and mix thoroughly. This will help bind the ingredients together.
  3. Add the condiments listed above to the meat and veg mixture: garlic powder, soy sauce, oyster sauce, five spice powder, salt and pepper. Combine thoroughly.
  4. Separate the spring roll wrappers. Place in lightly damp dish cloth to prevent the wrappers from drying out. While doing this step, the filling mixture will have time to marinade.
  5. Before filling the rolls, make sure you do a test taste first! Heat a small pan and add some oil. Once heated, scoop a tiny amount of the meat mixture and fry until brown. Check that you are happy with the seasoning and adjust accordingly.
  6. Scoop 1 tbsp of the meat and veg mixture and spread onto a wrapper.
  7. Fold the wrapper and form into a roll. Watch this tutorial.
  8. Heat the vegetable oil. To test if the oil is hot enough, grab a chopstick and stick in the middle of the pan. If it bubbles, the oil is ready.
  9. Fry the lumpia rolls until golden. Do not over crowd the pan.
  10. Once it is no longer boiling hot, dip the lumpia in sweet chilli sauce and enjoy!
